About Me

A little bit about myself and my career

I grew up in the Indian state of West Bengal where I often had to travel due to my parents job profile. This allowed me to learn and appreciate adapting and thriving in different enviroments I have always been fascinated by microorganisms ever since I learned about the world of microbes that exists hidden from the naked eye. This led me to pursue my B.Sc and M.Sc in Microbiology from St. Xaviers' College, Kolkata where I graduated with a GPA of 8.27 and 8.38 (out of 10), respectively. During my time there I worked on bioremediation of arsenic and nickel from groundwater where my team and I developed composite systems to efficiently and sustainably remove these toxic contaminants from wastewater. My roles in these projects involved handling and culturing of several microbes and other things.

During this time I also became aware of the critical state of accessibility to drinking water in many developing countries including my own, and this motivated me to pursue my PhD towards development of cheap, efficient and robust water decontamination systems. With this goal in mind, I joined IIT Bombay in 2019 after a brief stint teaching underpriviledged children in my hometown. Here, i was awarded the Prime Minister's research Fellowship in 2020 and currently I am working with Prof. R Bandopadhaya, to understand the interactions between bacteria and bactericidal nanoparticles at the micron level, using microfluidic devices; I finally wish to apply this knowledge to improve the efficiency of gravity based water purification columns and other cheap water purification devices.
